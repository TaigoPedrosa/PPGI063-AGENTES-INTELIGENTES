# Timeline - Intelligent Agents (1950–2000)

> The historical arc of intelligent agents, told across three team presentations.
> Team: Fernando Souza, Ruth Maria, Victor Lemos, Vinícius Sousa.

This section reconstructs **half a century of agents** in three eras. Each era has its own
deck, its own summary pages, and its own source PDF; this page is the map that connects
them.

## The through-line

A single argument runs across the decades:

> General-purpose reasoning is not enough on its own. Capability comes from the right mix of
> **knowledge, architecture, learning, and grounding in the world**, and the field keeps
> swinging between **symbolic** (reason about a model of the world) and **reactive/embodied**
> (let the world be its own model) answers.

- **1950–1970** establishes the **symbolic** bet: intelligence as search and explicit
  knowledge. Its limit is combinatorial explosion and brittleness.
- **1980–1990** tries to fix that with **cognitive architectures** and the **agent**
  abstraction, while Brooks opens the **reactive** counter-paradigm. The tension is
  unresolved, and hype collapses into the **AI Winter**.
- **1990–2000** turns the *single* agent into a **society of agents**. The question shifts
  from "how does one agent think?" to "how do many agents **coordinate and communicate**?",
  answered by the Contract Net Protocol, Agent-Oriented Programming, the communication
  languages KQML and FIPA-ACL, and the JADE platform that made them runnable.
