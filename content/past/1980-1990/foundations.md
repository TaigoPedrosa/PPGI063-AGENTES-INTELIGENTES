# Foundations - Intelligent Agents (1980–1990)

> Overview page derived from the team's presentation:
> [Intelligent Agents (1980–1990)](pdfs/intelligent-agents-1980-1990.pdf) (37 slides).
> Team: Fernando Souza, Ruth Maria, Victor Lemos, Vinícius Sousa.

If the **1950–1970** era invented symbolic AI, the **1980s** professionalized it, and then
fractured it. This decade is defined by two parallel stories: the **architecture of the
artificial mind** (cognitive architectures and the agent abstraction) and the **paradigm
clash** between top-down symbolic reasoning and bottom-up reactive behavior that ended in
the **AI Winter**.

---

## The central shift

Intelligence stops being framed only as theorem proving and becomes framed as an **agent**:

> Russell & Norvig recast intelligence as a **dynamic process**, where rational action
> rests on a continuous cycle of **perception through sensors** and **response through
> actuators**.

At the same time, AI **institutionalizes**: the first **AAAI conference (1980)** and the
**IJCAI** consolidate AI as an academic discipline, standardizing systems and globalizing
research collaboration.

---

## The four threads of this era

The presentation is organized into four themes, each with its own summary page:

1. **The Architecture of the Artificial Mind** — the agent definition, the
   institutionalization of AI, the two competing currents (expert systems vs. reactive
   agents), and the **ACT-R** cognitive architecture grounded in psychology and validated
   with fMRI.
2. **The SOAR Cognitive Architecture** — Newell, Laird & Rosenbloom's (1983) unified theory
   of cognition: reasoning as search in a problem space, plus **chunking** as automatic,
   experience-based learning.
3. **BDI (Belief–Desire–Intention)** — Bratman's (1987) practical-reasoning model for
   software agents, its **PRS** implementation, and the famous deployment monitoring
   NASA's Space Shuttle.
4. **Production Systems & the Reactive Critique** — the commercial peak of symbolic AI
   (OPS-5, R1/XCON) and Rodney Brooks's rupture: *"the world is its own best model"*,
   the subsumption architecture, and the resulting paradigm war.

---

## The tension that defines the decade

| Top-Down (Symbolic) | Bottom-Up (Reactive) |
| --- | --- |
| Declarative ("what") + procedural ("how") memory | Sensors and actuators |
| Planning and predefined goals | Fast response to external stimuli |
| Internal world model / representation | "The world is its own best model" |
| Expert systems, SOAR, ACT-R, BDI | Brooks's subsumption architecture |

Both approaches scored real wins, R1/XCON saved millions; subsumption robots reacted in
real time, but the **brittleness of rule-based symbolic AI** and inflated expectations
triggered the **AI Winter**, the cooling of funding and hype that closes the decade.
